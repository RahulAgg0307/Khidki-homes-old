11 Ways to design your Home in the Traditional Chettinad (Karaikudi) Style

Your Home is a place where you can feel like yourself, it’s your safe haven. The place where you create beautiful memories with your family and friends. If you love old Chettinad traditional architecture. Here are a few design elements you can include in your Home Interiors from this Blog.

1. Athangudi Tiles Flooring

Atangudi Tiles are cement tiles that have a unique property of cooling effect during summers and maintain warmth during winter months. Athangudi tiles were used in Chettinad Homes to serve this purpose. Athangudi Tiles tiles are suitable for all kinds of conditions, hence can be used Indoors as well as Outdoors. Athangudi tiles are eco-friendly and handmade, and for these reasons, they are a bit expensive compared to other flooring materials. Athangudi Tiles can be custom-made into any geometric pattern, floral design, or mosaic as per your need. Athangudi Tiles can also be used for Kitchen Backsplash. Some of the commonly available sizes in the market are 8*8 inches and 10*10 inches.

2. Terracotta Pillars

The ancient Tamil Nadu Homes had terracotta pillars with intricate carvings used for the courtyard boundary, creating a passageway to the rooms and the rest of the home. These days, it’s difficult to have a central courtyard due to the smaller spaces in the apartments. Instead, you can get a wooden carved pillar and get it installed in your apartment living room to give the traditional look and feel of Chettinad.

3. Wooden Windows & Doors

Decorative Hand Carved Wooden Doors with Iron Grills for Safety were used commonly in most Homes. You can use Hand Carved Wooden Doors and Windows instead of UPVC doors and windows in your Apartment.

4. Brass Decor Items

All South Indian communities used lamps in one way or the other in earlier times. Christians used it as Brass Candle Holders. Hindus used it as lamps, decor items, or God Figurines.

5. Swings

Oonjal or Swings were commonly used in the courtyard or living room of the home. In older times traditional wealthy families of South India had bigger open spaces, where they kept Swings. You can still spot swings in a few apartments of traditional families.

6. Paintings

Traditional Tanjore Paintings / Foil Madurai Paintings were quite popular in traditional south Indian homes. These paintings are still kept in home temples.

7. Sofa & Furniture

Traditional Sofa was made in Solid wood like teakwood, Sheesham, mahogany, rosewood, etc along with Rattan, Cane, Bamboo, etc. These days plywood, HDHMR, etc are used in making Furniture. You can still opt for solid wood Furniture if cost is not a constraint.

8. Hindu Temples

Big Temples with excessive wood carving on Huge Doors and Entrances was done in the old Chettinad Homes. These days, due to high costs and space constraints, people have started building smaller versions of these temples.

9. Bharanis or Big Jars

Big jars were used to store and preserve pickles. In modern times, Bharani has now become a decor item. You can use it as a flower Vase, a Planter placed indoors, or just like a decor element.

10. Wooden Rafters on Ceiling

Wooden Rafters were put on the ceiling to keep it cold during summers and warm during the winter. The wooden Rafters trend is now picking up in the interior world.

11. Canopy Beds

Canopy beds were used in traditional south Indian homes. Canopy Beds were used along with certain fabrics for temperature regulation. They added a great aesthetic value as well to the home.
